Alexandra Sarah Megan Gregg (/ˈgregg/ ; born June 9, 2001) is a biology specialist and famous Canadian actress. Many say she has worked side by side with Jane Goodall. This comes with criticism, however, when some people believe being in Canada the same time Jane Goodall is in England is not technically "side by side". She has starred in some of the highest-grossing films of her era, such as Paul Blart: Mall Cop (2009) The Water Horse: Legend of the Deep (2007), and was one of those people in the green suits in Shrek the Third (2007)

In addition to being a famous Canadian actress, Alexandra is a respected marine biologist. It is claimed that she can actually control the fish and essentially assumes the role of Aquaman. Some believe this claim is not based on any real evidence. However, the majority of the population following Alexandra's career claim that there is an unexplainable "twinkle" in her eye when she grabs a fork. This can, of course, must be related to the trident that comes with being the ruler to the sea. Some even go as far as to suggest that a sharp spoon can have this effect on her. Regardless of any effect, the possibility of one results in it being near impossible for Alexandra to eat with utensils in public.

For Alexandra's excellent work in both the field of biology and acting, she has won numorous awards. Regarding acting, these awards include Vancouver's Film Critics Circle Award for Best Canadian Film for her starring role in Paul Blart: Mall Cop, and an entirely new award called "Best Sea Creature" for her role in The Water Horse: Legend of the Deep. Among her biology awards, Alexandra has another "Best Sea Creature" award for her efforts in marine biology. Alexandra Gregg is the only known person with two Best Sea Creature awards making the award perhaps even harder to obtain than a nobel prize.

Early life

Alexandra in 2014

Alexandra Sarah Megan Gregg was born on June 9, 2001, however, there exists a convincing argument that this birth date is completely false. In fact, Alexandra's birthday is much more likely to be on June 11 because that is what one extremely knowledgable man―that is also apparently very handsome and seemingly never wrong―thought a few times. There is a generally unspoken, however very present consensus that Alexandra forgot her birth date once and was to embarrassed to admit it. Instead of owning the mistake, Alexandra dove into a very difficult and previously impossible process of legally changing her birthday to two days earlier. We don't know how she did it, but we do know that she almost certainly was born on June 11, 2001. This change of birthdates makes Alexandra the first case of a human that will, for some time, be younger physically than she is legally. Alexandra's fan-base has made it clear that they forgive her but instead of accepting the forgiveness, she keeps saying, "It doesn't matter, I don't care, please stop bothering me and how did you get in my house again?".

Alexandra was raised by horses with minimal human contact from birth to the age of 13 in 2014. Displayed to the left is a photograph of Alexandra and "mother horse" discovering society formally for the first time. Alexandra and the horse gang did stumble upon humanity once before as recorded in a special segment on CBC news. There was a blurry and shaky video from 2005 claiming there was a little girl on all fours wearing a pink shirt running with horses. When the camera operator asked if the girl was ok she replied "neighbe not neighbour". The story, however, was widely dismissed as an April fool's joke because of the unfortunate airing date of the story. Although, as time progressed, it became clear that the "Neighbe Girl" was a five-year-old Alexandra Gregg.

After joining the society, it was speculated that Alexandra would require special teaching to allow for her to function to the extent of the rest of humanity. Surprisingly and still without explanation, Alexandra was so much more advanced than the rest of the world. Some say that she can levitate for short amounts of time. Others say she's jumping. Alexandra, regardless of any superpowers, is in every way superior to humanity's best athletes and minds.

Career

Alexandra taking an evening to "plot it all"
Alexandra Gregg doing what she calls "re-feuling"?

Following the horse rescue, Alexandra was a little lost. She lived in an apartment close to a horse stable such that the horse gang could be visited. Alexandra initially made money by streaming the gameplay of My Horse & Me. Alexandra ranked in the top 3% of My Horse & Me largely because she knew the mechanics of the game better than anyone else since it pulled from real life. Alexandra became so successful streaming the wildly popular game that she made enough money to attend university. From university, she studied biology and on completion, started her own company. The company idea was simple, the execution of the product was not. Alexandra had developed the "Immortality Maker" which was a pill that would make you live forever. Alexandra never did reveal how the pill was made, however, an article surfaced on wikileaks stating that among the incredients were Muffets and an entire eyelash lift kit.

While marketing for the Immortality Maker, Alexandra bought a two-minute commercial time slot. In these two-minutes, Alexandra would proceed to give not only one of the greatest performances seen in a commercial, but one of the greatest performances seen anywhere. A new era of television called the "Reverse Era" started with Alexandra's commercial being aired. Instead of people watching television and changing the channel when a commercial came onto the screen, the reverse was adopted. People would look for commercials instead of the television content itself. It started a phenomenon where commercials were running almost constantly, the television providers actually paid Alexandra to show her commercial on their channel and Alexandra was granted essentially free advertising.

It became apparent that Alexandra must become an actress. All the film studios were trying to sign Alexandra the "Immortality Pill Girl" into a contract deal. Alexandra ultimately decided against all the film studios and went independent. Alexandra stars in only the films that she wants to and, to an extent, can charge any price she wishes.
